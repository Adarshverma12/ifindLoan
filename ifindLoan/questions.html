Your Personal Loan Questions, Answered

Clear answers about how iFindLoan works, what it costs, credit impact, funding speed, and how your information is protected.

Everything people ask most about using iFindLoan, in one place. Still stuck? Reach us on our Contact page.

What is iFindLoan?

iFindLoan is a free loan-matching service. We take one short request and connect it with lenders and lending partners in our network who may offer a personal loan from $100 to $5,000. We are not a lender.

Is iFindLoan a lender?

No. iFindLoan does not lend money or make credit decisions. The lender you are matched with writes the offer, sets the rate, and funds the loan.

How much can I request?

Between $100 and $5,000. The amount you are offered depends on the lender, your state, and your financial profile.

How much does it cost to use iFindLoan?

Nothing. iFindLoan is always free to consumers. Lenders may compensate us for connecting you, but that never adds a fee to your loan.

Will submitting a request affect my credit score?

Submitting your request to iFindLoan does not affect your credit score. A lender may perform a credit check when you continue with a specific offer; the lender discloses that step and its impact.

Can I get matched with bad credit?

All credit types are welcome to submit a request. Many lenders consider income and employment alongside credit history. Submitting a request does not guarantee an offer or approval.

How fast will I receive funds?

If you accept a lender's offer, funds are often deposited by direct deposit as soon as the next business day. Exact timing depends on the lender and your bank.

Is my information secure?

Your request is protected with 256-bit SSL encryption in transit and handled per our Privacy Policy. You can opt out of the sale or sharing of your data and unsubscribe at any time.

Do I have to accept an offer?

No. You are never obligated to accept. If no offer fits, you can walk away at no cost.

Is iFindLoan available in every state?

No. The service is not available in all states. If loans are prohibited in your state, or no lender is available there, you will not be connected to a lender.

What are tribal lenders?

Some lenders in the network are tribal lenders operating under tribal law. Their rates and fees may be higher than state-licensed lenders. Always review the lender's terms.

How do I stop receiving messages?

Visit our Unsubscribe page to opt out of emails, or reply STOP to any text message. You can also submit a Do Not Sell My Info request.

Important Disclosures. Please Read Carefully.

This service is not available in all states. If you request a loan in a particular state where such loans are prohibited, or in a location where ifindloan.com does not have an available lender, you will not be connected to a lender. In some cases, you may be given the option of obtaining a loan from a tribal lender. Tribal lenders are subject to tribal and certain federal laws while being immune from state law including usury caps. If you are connected to a tribal lender, please understand that the tribal lender's rates and fees may be higher than state-licensed lenders.

The purpose of shorter duration loans is to provide the borrower temporary financial relief. Such loans are not a long-term financial solution. Persons facing serious financial difficulties should consider other alternatives or should seek out professional financial advice.

This website is not an offer to lend. ifindloan.com is not a lender and does not make loan or credit decisions. ifindloan.com connects interested persons with a lender from its network of approved lenders. ifindloan.com does not control and is not responsible for the actions or inactions of any lender. Regardless, ifindloan.com's service is always free to you.

By submitting your information via this website, you are authorizing ifindloan.com and its partners to do a credit check, which may include verifying your social security number, driver license number or other identification, and a review of your creditworthiness.

Lender's Disclosure of Terms.

The lender you are connected to will provide documents that contain all fees and rate information pertaining to the loan being offered. Loan fees and interest rates are determined solely by the lender based on the lender's internal policies, underwriting criteria and applicable law.

Late Payments Hurt Your Credit Score

Please be aware that missing a payment or making a late payment can negatively impact your credit score.
