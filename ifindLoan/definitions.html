Personal Loan Terms & Definitions

APR, principal, origination fee, term — a glossary of the words you will meet in a personal loan offer, explained simply.

Loans come with a lot of jargon. Here are the terms you are most likely to meet, in plain English. iFindLoan is a matching service, not a lender — these definitions are educational and are not financial advice.

Loan terms glossary

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The yearly cost of a loan including interest and certain fees, shown as a percentage. Best single number for comparing offers of the same length.

Principal

The amount you actually borrow, before interest. On a $1,000 loan, the principal is $1,000.

Interest

The cost a lender charges for borrowing, usually calculated as a percentage of the outstanding principal.

Term

How long you have to repay the loan, often expressed in months. A longer term lowers the monthly payment but can raise total interest.

Origination fee

A one-time fee some lenders charge to process a loan, often taken from the loan amount as a percentage of the principal.

Installment loan

A loan repaid in fixed, scheduled payments (installments) over a set term. Most personal loans are installment loans.

Direct deposit

Electronic transfer of funds straight into your bank account, the way most matched loans are funded.

Prequalification

An early estimate of what a lender might offer, based on limited information. It is not a guaranteed offer or approval.

Soft vs. hard credit inquiry

A soft inquiry does not affect your credit score; a hard inquiry may lower it slightly and happens when a lender formally reviews your application.

Cosigner

A second person who agrees to repay the loan if you cannot. A cosigner can help some applicants qualify.

Default

Failing to repay a loan according to its terms. Default can seriously damage your credit and lead to collection activity.

Tribal lender

A lender owned by or affiliated with a Native American tribe, operating under tribal law. Rates and fees may be higher than state-licensed lenders.

Ping-tree / marketplace

A system that offers your request to multiple lenders in sequence so the best available match can connect with you.

Rollover / refinance

Replacing or extending an existing loan with a new one. This can add cost; review terms carefully before agreeing.
