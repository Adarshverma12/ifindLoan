Personal Loan Rates & Fees, Explained

iFindLoan does not set rates or charge fees. Lenders do. This page explains how personal loan costs work so you can read any offer with confidence.

iFindLoan is a free matching service and does not set interest rates, APRs, or fees. Those are determined solely by the lender that makes you an offer. This page is a plain-English guide to the costs you may see so you can compare offers wisely.

APR: the number that matters most

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) reflects the yearly cost of a loan including interest and certain fees, expressed as a percentage. Because it bundles more than the interest rate alone, APR is usually the best single number for comparing two offers of the same term.

Personal loan APRs vary widely based on your state, credit profile, income, loan amount, and the lender's policies. Well-qualified borrowers typically see lower APRs; higher-risk profiles see higher ones. Some lenders in the network — including tribal lenders — may offer rates that are higher than state-licensed lenders.

Common fees to look for

FeeWhat it is
Origination feeA one-time charge some lenders take from the loan amount to process it. Often expressed as a percentage of the principal.
Late payment feeCharged if a payment arrives after its due date. Amounts and grace periods vary by lender and state.
Returned payment feeCharged if a payment fails (for example, insufficient funds).
Prepayment penaltyA charge for paying the loan off early. Many lenders do not charge this — check the offer.

How to keep your cost down

  • Borrow only what you need and choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford.
  • Compare the APR, not just the monthly payment — a lower payment over a longer term can cost more overall.
  • Read the lender's full terms for origination, late, and prepayment details before you sign.
  • Only accept a loan you can repay on schedule. Late payments can hurt your credit score.
